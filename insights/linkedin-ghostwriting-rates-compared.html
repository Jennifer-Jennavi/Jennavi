The complete rate breakdown for every model of LinkedIn ghostwriting in 2026. What each level actually buys you, where the value breaks down, and exactly where Jennavi sits in the market.

What you are actually paying for at each level

The freelancer model: $50 to $400 per month

Freelancers occupy the widest range of quality in the LinkedIn ghostwriting market. At $50 to $150 per month you are almost certainly paying for AI-generated content with light human editing. The posts will be coherent. They will not be strategic. They will not sound like you. And they will not convert to inbound leads because they lack the positioning specificity that makes a founder's content recognisable and trusted.

At $200 to $400 per month, freelancers can be genuinely skilled. Some are former content strategists, former journalists, former marketers who understand LinkedIn. The problem at this level is not talent. It is accountability. Freelancers have multiple clients, variable workloads, and no formal methodology to apply to your positioning. You are buying their time, not their system.

The right question to ask any freelancer is not "how much do you charge?" but "show me three posts you wrote for three different founders that sound like three completely different people." If they all sound the same, the rate is irrelevant.

The boutique specialist model: $200 to $800 per month

A boutique specialist is a single expert who writes your content personally, applies a documented methodology to your positioning, and treats your LinkedIn as a business asset rather than a content calendar. At this level, you are paying for strategic depth that a freelancer cannot consistently deliver and a personal relationship that an agency cannot provide.

The difference between a $200 per month freelancer and a $200 per month boutique specialist is the methodology. A boutique specialist at $200 per month has a positioning framework, a voice-capture process, and a defined theory of why certain content converts on LinkedIn. A freelancer at $200 per month has availability and a writing skill.

The CRICKETS framework underpins every post. And the results are documented publicly. 873% impression growth, 6,000+ followers, 106,000+ organic impressions, zero paid ads.

The mid-market agency model: $1,500 to $4,000 per month

Mid-market agencies provide a team structure: an account manager who handles your relationship, a content strategist who builds your quarterly plan, and a junior writer who produces your content. The account manager sounds senior. The strategist sounds experienced. But the person writing your LinkedIn posts is almost always someone you have never spoken to.

This is the most common source of founder disappointment in LinkedIn ghostwriting. A founder pays $2,000 to $3,000 per month for three to six months and produces content that is technically proficient but completely generic. The content sounds like it could belong to any founder in their industry. That is because it was written by someone who knows the industry in general but does not know you specifically.

Mid-market agency rates are worth paying when the agency has genuine expertise in your specific industry vertical and when the writing team has been briefed on your positioning at depth. For most founders in B2B professional services, the boutique specialist delivers better results at a fraction of the cost.

The premium agency model: $4,000 to $8,000 per month

Premium agencies provide everything: strategy, content, design, light outbound, LinkedIn ad management, analytics dashboards, quarterly business reviews. They are appropriate for founders who have tested LinkedIn as a channel, proven it converts for their business, and want to scale the operation professionally.

At this level, you are paying for capacity and infrastructure, not just writing. The rate reflects a team of five to ten people each contributing to your LinkedIn presence. For founders at growth or scale stage who have already validated their positioning and simply need more, this investment can generate strong returns. For founders still at the stage of establishing their presence and testing their positioning, it is a significant investment for a problem that a boutique specialist can solve for a tenth of the cost.

The comparison that actually matters: what converts

Rate comparisons are useful but they miss the variable that matters most: whether the content generates inbound leads. A founder spending $400 per month on content that generates zero inbound is spending more than a founder spending $400 per month on content that generates two qualified conversations per month. The rate is not the number that matters. The output is.

Signs a model is not worth the rate
  • Content sounds generic across three different clients
  • No documented methodology or positioning framework
  • Junior writer who has never spoken to you
  • Account manager is the only person who knows your business
  • Monthly reporting shows impressions but not inbound inquiries
  • Content reads the same month after month without evolution
  • Ghostwriter has no active LinkedIn presence themselves
Signs a model is worth every cent
  • Three posts from three clients sound like three different people
  • Documented framework with named methodology
  • The person writing knows your business directly
  • Voice-capture process includes multiple conversations
  • Reporting includes inbound inquiry frequency
  • Content evolves as your positioning sharpens
  • Ghostwriter has documented their own LinkedIn growth
