About This Tool

Understanding
Your Scores

What is the difference between Hook Audit and Content Audit?
Hook Audit scores only your opening line out of 100 across four CRICKETS hook dimensions: Recognition, Discomfort, Curiosity, and Surprise. Each dimension scores up to 25 points. Use it before writing your full post to test and sharpen the first line. Content Audit scores your full post out of 100 across Hook Quality, Pain Specificity, Proof Presence, and Conversion Intent, with additional length and formatting scoring. Use it on a completed post before you publish. Both modes are equally important. The hook determines whether anyone reads the rest. The content determines whether anyone reaches out after reading.
What makes a LinkedIn hook stop the scroll?
A hook stops the scroll when it creates one of four immediate emotional responses before the reader makes a conscious decision. Recognition: this is exactly my situation. Discomfort: I have been avoiding thinking about this. Curiosity: I need to know where this is going. Surprise: I did not expect that. The CRICKETS framework from the manifesto by Jennifer Omaliko identifies these four as the only scroll-stoppers that consistently work. Opening with I centres the writer not the reader, which is the most common hook failure. Generic questions ask for effort before delivering value, which is the second most common failure.
Why does my post get views but not generate clients?
Views without clients is the Applause Economy problem. Your content is playing the Visibility game instead of the Conversion game. The CRICKETS framework identifies two games on LinkedIn. Game One produces impressions and applause. Game Two produces client conversations and revenue. The most common causes of high views with zero clients are: educational posts that name no specific client pain, posts that end without a clear single next step, and posts that contain no specific proof or numbers. Run your post through the Content Audit. The Conversion Intent score will tell you exactly what is missing.
What is the optimal length for a LinkedIn post in 2026?
The optimal length for a LinkedIn post in 2026 is 1300 to 1900 characters. Under 300 characters is too short for LinkedIn to treat as substantial content and receives a 20-point deduction. Under 800 characters receives a 10-point deduction. Over 3000 characters reads as an article and most readers never click See More to reach the rest of the post, receiving a 20-point deduction. The Content Audit scores your post length transparently and shows exactly which deduction applied and why.
What is Pain Specificity and why is it scored?
Pain Specificity measures whether your post names the specific private version of your client's problem rather than the general category. The CRICKETS Manifesto calls this the Bleeding Neck principle. Vitamins are optional. Painkillers are not. A post that says "LinkedIn can be challenging for founders" names a category. A post that names the specific moment a founder watches a less qualified competitor land the speaking slot they wanted, and traces that moment directly back to their LinkedIn presence, names the bleeding neck. The second version converts. The first does not. Pain Specificity rewards posts that name the 2am version of the problem.
