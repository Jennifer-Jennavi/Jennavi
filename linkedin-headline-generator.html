What makes a great LinkedIn headline for founders?
A great LinkedIn headline names a specific result for a specific client without using your job title. It answers the question every profile visitor is silently asking: what changes for me if I work with this person? The best headlines combine who you serve, what outcome you produce, and one proof element or differentiator. Job titles like CEO or Founder tell the visitor nothing about your value.
How does the LinkedIn Headline Generator work?
Enter what you do, who you serve, and your best result. The generator creates five headlines using five different CRICKETS positioning formulas: the Result-First formula leads with your outcome, the Client-Specific formula speaks directly to your ideal buyer, the Authority and Proof formula uses your documented evidence, the Problem-Solution formula names the exact pain you fix, and the Identity and Outcome formula positions you as the definitive answer. Each formula creates a distinctly different angle on the same positioning.
Should I use my job title in my LinkedIn headline?
No. Your job title is what LinkedIn fills in by default when you do nothing. It communicates your role but not your value. A positioning-led headline that names your target client and the result you produce will always outperform a job title. A visitor who sees their exact problem or desired outcome named in your headline is far more likely to connect, message, or book a call than a visitor who sees Founder or CEO.
How long should my LinkedIn headline be?
LinkedIn allows up to 220 characters for your headline. The sweet spot is 120 to 180 characters. Long enough to include your client, your result, and a differentiator. Short enough to be read in a single scan. Headlines that are too short miss the opportunity to communicate value. Headlines that are too long lose the reader before they finish. The five headlines this tool generates are all optimised for this range.
