Understanding
Your Hooks

What is a LinkedIn hook and why does it matter?
A LinkedIn hook is the first one or two lines of your post that appear before the See More button. It is the only part of your post that is visible without clicking. If the hook does not stop the scroll, nobody reads the rest. Over 80% of LinkedIn users never click See More on posts whose first line does not immediately interest them. Your hook is not just important. It is everything.
What are the four CRICKETS hook types?
Recognition hooks make the reader feel immediately seen and understood. They name a specific experience or frustration so precisely that the reader thinks you wrote the post about them. Discomfort hooks name a painful truth the reader has been avoiding. They create urgency by surfacing consequences the reader has been ignoring. Curiosity hooks create an information gap the reader feels compelled to close. They withhold something the reader wants to know. Surprise hooks challenge a belief the reader holds as true. They stop the scroll by contradicting an assumption.
Which hook type should I use most often?
The best LinkedIn content strategies rotate through all four types. Recognition builds connection and trust. Discomfort drives action and urgency. Curiosity generates clicks and saves. Surprise creates shares and debate. Using only one type makes your content predictable and your engagement will plateau. Jennifer's ghostwriting clients use all four in a structured rotation that keeps their audience engaged across every stage of the buyer journey.
